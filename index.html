About Kinwani House

❮
❯

Nestled amidst the breathtaking landscapes of Narendra Nagar, the erstwhile capital of the princely state of Tehri Garhwal, lies the enchanting Kinwani House. This architectural gem stands as a testament to the rich history and cultural heritage of the region. Kinwani House serves not only as a physical structure but also as a portal to the past, offering visitors a glimpse into the grandeur of a bygone era.

Narendra Nagar, once the princely state of Tehri Garhwal and later chosen as the summer retreat by the British, exudes an old-world charm that captivates visitors. Its regal architecture, lush gardens, and panoramic vistas serve as a testament to its royal heritage. Named after the illustrious King Narendra Shah of Tehri, the town carries echoes of a bygone era, inviting exploration into its storied past. Both Narendra Nagar and Rishikesh share a deep connection to spirituality with countless temples, ashrams and sacred sites dotting their landscapes. From the iconic Parmarth Niketan Ashram and the ancient Triveni Ghat in Rishikesh to the serene Kunjapuri Devi Temple overlooking Narendra Nagar, these towns serve as sanctuaries for introspection, renewal, and inner peace.

One remarkable aspect of Kinwani House is its geographical significance, with its longitude nearly aligning with 79 degrees east. This longitude holds special significance for Hindus, as it passes through all twelve Shiva temples, from the sacred shrine of Kedarnath in the north to the southern tip of Kanyakumari. This alignment further enhances the spiritual aura of Kinwani House, making it a place of reverence and contemplation for pilgrims and leisure travellers alike.
